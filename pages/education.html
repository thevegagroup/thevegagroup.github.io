Training Program

The Cornell Data Science training program is a resource intended for anyone interested in learning how to work with, analyze, and make predictions with data. No prior data science experience is required.

The program introduces various machine learning models, visualization techniques, and data manipulation strategies, with applications in the R programming language. When you finish this program, you will have the foundation and basic skills to contribute to any subteam in Cornell Data Science. Pre-requisites: We recommend you be currently enrolled, or have previously taken an intro to computer science course (CS 1110 or equivalent), though it is not strictly required to succeed in the training program.

FAQ

How long and frequent are lectures?
Lectures will be 1 hour in length and will be once per week. We will meet on Wednesdays at 5:00pm. Additionally, any homeworks or project assignments will be due the night before the following week's session.

Can I take this program for credit?
Trainees have the option to receive 1 S/U credit through the Cornell Data Science project team.

Do I have to be a certain year or in a certain school to apply?
There is no major or year requirement to apply. Students enrolled in any school are welcome!
